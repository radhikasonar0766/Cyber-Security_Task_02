**1. Confidentiality**

**Definition:** Confidentiality means protecting information from unauthorized access. Only authorized people should be able to view sensitive data.



**Why it is Important:**

Protects personal and sensitive information.

Prevents data theft and privacy violations.

Maintains trust between organizations and users.



**Real-World Example:**

Only authorized staff members can access student examination records in a college database.



**2. Integrity**

**Definition:** Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, complete, and unaltered.



**Why it is Important:**

Prevents unauthorized modification of data.

Maintains data accuracy.

Helps organizations make correct decisions.



**Real-World Example:**

Student marks should not be changed by unauthorized users.



**3. Availability**

**Definition**: Availability ensures that systems and data are accessible whenever authorized users need them.



**Why it is Important:**

Supports continuous operations.

Prevents service interruptions.

Ensures users can access resources on time.



**Real-World Example:**

A college website should remain available during online admission periods.
