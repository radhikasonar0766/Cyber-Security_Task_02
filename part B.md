**WannaCry Ransomware Case Study**



**What Happened**:

The WannaCry ransomware attack began on 12 May 2017 and quickly spread across the world, infecting more than 200,000 computers in over 150 countries. The attackers exploited a security vulnerability in Microsoft Windows called EternalBlue, which targeted the Server Message Block (SMB) protocol. Once a computer was infected, the ransomware encrypted all important files and displayed a message demanding payment in Bitcoin to restore access to the data. The malware spread automatically across networks, allowing it to infect multiple computers within organizations. Many hospitals, businesses, government agencies, and educational institutions were affected. One of the major victims was the United Kingdom's National Health Service (NHS), where hospitals had to cancel appointments and delay medical services because their computer systems became inaccessible.



**Which Part of the CIA Triad Was Affected:**

WannaCry mainly affected the Availability component of the CIA Triad because users lost access to their files, applications, and computer systems. Critical systems became unavailable, disrupting normal operations. The attack also affected Integrity because files were modified and encrypted without the owner's permission. In some cases, Confidentiality was also at risk because attackers gained unauthorized access to systems. However, the most severely affected component was availability, as organizations could not access their important data and services until the systems were restored.



**What Was the Impact:**

The WannaCry attack caused massive financial and operational damage worldwide. Thousands of organizations experienced service interruptions, data loss, and productivity issues. Hospitals canceled surgeries and appointments, businesses temporarily shut down operations, and government offices experienced system failures. The attack caused losses estimated in billions of dollars due to recovery costs, downtime, and damaged systems. Many organizations had to rebuild their networks, restore data from backups, and strengthen their security infrastructure. The incident also increased awareness about the importance of cybersecurity and the dangers of outdated software.



**How Could It Have Been Prevented:**

The WannaCry attack could have been prevented through several cybersecurity practices. Microsoft had already released a security patch before the attack, but many organizations failed to install it. Regular operating system updates and patch management would have closed the vulnerability used by the attackers. Organizations should maintain regular backups so that encrypted data can be restored without paying ransom. Installing and updating antivirus software can help detect malware before it spreads. Firewalls and network security tools can block malicious traffic, while employee cybersecurity training can help users recognize suspicious activities. Implementing strong security policies and continuously monitoring systems can greatly reduce the risk of similar ransomware attacks in the future.
